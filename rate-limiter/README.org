#+title: Rate Limiter

A rate limiter is one of the best systems to learn distributed systems thinking because it touches:
- scheduling
- fairness
- contention
- time
- queues
- memory
- consistency
- distributed coordination
- backpressure
- failure handling

Rate limiting is fundamentally about controlling resource allocation under contention.

* What is a rate limiter?
A rate limiter is a control plane that decides whether work is allowed to proceed. Usually based on:
- time
- identity
- resource usage
- concurrency
- quotas
* Mental Model
** Parts of a Rate Limiter 
Each rate limtier has 3 components/parts:
1. Identity - Who are we limiting?
2. Policy - What are the rules?
3. State Store - Where are we tracking usage?
** Types of Rate limiters
There are fundamentally only 2 types of rate limiters:
*** Request-count based
It controls how many operations happen over time. Example: 100 requests/minute.

There are various algorithms that can be used to implement this:
- Fixed window
- Sliding window
- Sliding log
- Token bucket
- Leaky bucket

This is what most people mean by "rate limiter."
*** Concurrency-based
It controls how many operations are simultaneously running. Examples:
- max 10 Puppeteer pages
- max 50 DB connections
- max 5 active jobs

This is actually closer to:
- semaphores
- worker pools
- admission control

** Problems
*** Missing Burst protection
All requests can be send in a burst instead of evenly spaced out.
*** Boundary burst issue
At the boundaries, the traffic can be send in bursts during window reset. So, at the boundary, 1 millisecond before can send all the traffic, then one millisecond after the window reset, can send another full burst of traffic.
*** Traffic stampede at reset
When users figure out when the reset is going to happen, everyone might start pressuring the system at the exact same time.
*** Sync in distributed systems
Since everyone must agree on the remaining count or window, it is hard to stay in sync because of memory contention issues.
*** Unfair
Users who start near the reset boundary get quota replishment faster as compared to those who arrive after the window resets.
